Cambridge Sustainable Design Group

The Cambridge Sustainable Design Group investigates the relationship between architectural design and planetary health. Our central belief is that buildings, neighbourhoods, and cities significantly shape human environmental exposure, behavioural patterns, and ultimately health and energy outcomes.

We integrate building physics, epidemiology, and behavioural science with computational technologies including machine learning and AI — developing evidence-based strategies for sustainable, healthy living environments through cross-disciplinary collaboration among architects, health scientists, and environmental specialists.

LSHTM Climate and Health Group

The Climate and Health Group at the London School of Hygiene & Tropical Medicine is an interdisciplinary group of researchers investigating the health impacts associated with climate variability and change. The group generates evidence on the health impacts of key climate hazards and the actions needed to protect public health.

The group hosts the NIHR Health Protection Research Unit on Climate Change and Health Security, working in collaboration with the UK Health Security Agency, University College London, and the Met Office to inform UK and global public health policy.
